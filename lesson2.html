Part 3: Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense

A. Regular Verbs (Add -ed)

We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that finished in the past. For regular verbs, we add -ed to the base form.

Rule 1: Most verbs

Add -ed to the base form. (e.g., work → worked)

Rule 2: Verbs ending in -e

Add only -d. (e.g., join → joined)

B. Irregular Verbs (Must Memorize)

Irregular verbs do not follow the -ed rule. Their past tense forms are unique. These are very common!

  • see → saw
  • make → made
  • go → went
